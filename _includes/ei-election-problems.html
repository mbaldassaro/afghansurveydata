BIGGEST PROBLEMS OF 2014 PRESIDENTIAL ELECTIONS

What was the main problem with the 2014 presidential election, if any?

A plurality of nearly thirty percent of respondents cited corruption in the Independent Election Commission as the biggest problem of the election. Following IEC corruption, the most significant problems reported were lack of ballots and voter fraud. Respondents in the north, southeast, and west were most likely to report lack of ballots as the main problem and respondents in the south, west, and northeast were most likely to report voter fraud as the main problem. A quarter of respondents in the east cited security as the biggest problem, which is far above the national average.

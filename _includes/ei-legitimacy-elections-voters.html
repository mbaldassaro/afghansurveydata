LEGITIMACY OF 2014 ELECTIONS ACCORDING TO VOTERS

Do you agree that the election result of the 2014 presidential election was legitimate?

Respondents who voted in the 2014 presidential election were more likely to agree that the result was legitimate. In the east, 80 percent of respondents agreed that the election result was legitimate. Among only respondents who reported voting in the election, 94 percent agreed the result was legitimate. In the north, 64 percent of respondents agreed that the result was legitimate. Among voters, that rate increases to 72 percent. This trend is consistent across the country.
